Importance of Enough and Quality Sleep

Please watch this Ted Talk by Russell Foster, who is a circadian neuroscientist. In this talk, Foster shares three popular theories about why we sleep, busts some myths about how much sleep we need at different ages.

Restoration

Essentially, all the stuff we've burned up during the day, we restore, we replace, we rebuild during the night. Within the brain, a whole raft of genes have been shown to be turned on only during sleep, and those genes are associated with restoration and metabolic pathways.

Energy conservation

You sleep to save calories. If you compare an individual who has slept at night, or stayed awake and hasn't moved very much, the energy saving of sleeping is about 110 calories a night.

Brain processing and memory consolidation

We have ability to come up with novel solutions to complex problems is hugely enhanced by a night of sleep. It's been estimated that sleaping can give us a threefold advantage. Sleeping at night enhances our creativity.
